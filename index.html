* Studied hard * Did best in high-school * At college, I just chose default Electrical Engineering (maybe you can choose Computer Science). * Once I got into college, it was Little bit more relaxed. Not as bad. I did okay. You might think everyone in IITs is super smart. But they are just normal people who worked super hard in high school. * IIT have placement season where all the companies come and interview back to back. Almost everyone gets a really good job. I had 6 back to back interviews 5 rejects and one accept. Any bank Once I paid off my student loans. * DEShaw Hyderabad: an IT role in a US-based financial company. Like most software workplaces, this english speaking worksplace. More of my colleagues did not know Hindi, so I had to speak English. I made a lot of money and quickly paid of my student loans. Eat out regularly, hire a maid, travel regularly, buy a bike; not having to think about small expenses. * Working at DeShaw is a desirable job. A lot of my coworkers were from Microsoft, Amazon, Google job or went there. * DeShaw was great for the first two years. At first there was a lot to learn and I was happy about that. Life was great, but the learning stopped. At workplace once you learn something, your boss will ask to do the same thing over and over again. I also managed to save a lot of money. So I want to try something new. While some of frieds applied to other companies, I applied for PhDs in the USA. * What is PhD? I would run into hard problems at work, but the company will not allow me to work on those. PhD is just like school but you are working full time on projects, no course work no exams, just projects; but really hard projects. Projects that no one has ever done before, not even your professor. * So I applied for 3 years straight to 10 colleges per year. I got one accept in the third try. I got into a US University. * PhD is usually required to become a professor. PhD in engineering usually takes 4-7 years. In PhD they pay you a little bit. It is all research projects and writing about them. On an average Masters students make more money than a PhD. If you care about making money then, you should do just Masters. PhD jobs are really. * US is a bit different then India. Yes, dollar to rupee conversion is high. Yes you make income in dollars. But living in the USA is also expensive. Unlike Deshaw eating out regulary was a luxury. I learned to cook and clean for the first time. After doing research in the USA I applied to lot of University for professorship in the USA and in India. After many more 40 rejections per year for 2 years, I got in this position. # Possible questions * What are some tips for clear entrance tests? * Why should one want to be a professor? * How are students in IIT like? * How hard do you need to work in IIT? * Should we fear robot takeover?

- Navigation is the problem of converting sequence of observation to a sequence of actions for the purpose of going from one place to another. - It is often addressed in three parts. - Mapping---which is the estimation of the static part of the environment. - Localization---which is the estimation of the dynamic state of the environment like the agents location in the map. - and Planning which is the estimation of the sequence of action that moves the agent from current state to the desired goal. + Most of my work has been focused around mapping with some work around localization and planning. + Today I am going to talk about three of my works. + First I am going to talk about my work on making mapping faster by using modern inference methods on factor graphs. + Then I am going to talk about mutual localiztion that intern enables faster mapping by allowing robots to divide and conquer the environment. + In the end I will talk about making goal conditioned reinforcement learning faster by removing redundant computation. +
